EFF CHAPTER LEADERSHIP TRAINING

Director of Collegiate Ministry Academy

A deep, practical formation experience for the leader helping students grow in faith, build community, and connect to the care they need. This is ministry leadership training—not clinical, legal, or professional counseling certification.

0 of 8 modules completed

Module 1 · Your ministry assignment

Clarify the purpose of the role: create welcoming pathways to faith, community, reflection, and practical EFF support. Build a one-sentence ministry vision for your chapter.

Module 2 · The semester ministry map

Plan a simple rhythm: one gathering, one discipleship touchpoint, one care check-in, and one service opportunity each month. Protect space for student feedback.

Module 3 · Events students actually attend

Use the event formula: clear purpose, welcoming invitation, student voices, one meaningful activity, practical next step, and follow-up within 48 hours.

Module 4 · Lead a Bible study

Prepare a passage, one historical context note, three open questions, a prayer prompt, and a practical action. Let students participate; do not lecture them through every answer.

Module 5 · Care with wisdom and boundaries

Listen, affirm, do not diagnose, do not promise secrecy you cannot keep, and know when to bring in an advisor, counselor, 988, emergency services, or EFF resource support.

Module 6 · Build a prayer and reflection experience

Create a safe flow: welcome, Scripture, quiet reflection, optional sharing, prayer, resource reminder, and a gentle closing. Never pressure students to disclose personal information.

Module 7 · Lead leaders and volunteers

Give people clear roles, a short pre-event huddle, an opening prayer, clear responsibilities, and a post-event debrief. Celebrate what worked and improve one thing.

Module 8 · Your 30-day ministry action plan

Choose one event, one Bible-study or reflection gathering, one care pathway, and one follow-up practice to launch in the next 30 days.
